# Script Hooks Database

## Educational/Tutorial Hooks

### Problem-Solution Hooks
1. "Every time you [specific action], you're probably [mistake]. Here's why."
2. "This one mistake costs [audience] [consequence] every single day."
3. "I spent [time period] struggling with [problem] until I discovered this."
4. "90% of people get this wrong. Are you one of them?"
5. "[Common belief] is completely wrong. Let me prove it."

### Curiosity Hooks  
1. "What if I told you [counterintuitive statement]?"
2. "There's a hidden feature in [tool/platform] that nobody talks about."
3. "The secret that [authority/experts] don't want you to know..."
4. "I accidentally discovered something that changed everything."
5. "This weird trick actually works, and here's the science."

### Results/Transformation Hooks
1. "In the next [time], you'll be able to [specific capability]."
2. "Watch me turn [before state] into [after state] in real-time."
3. "This method helped me [specific achievement] in just [timeframe]."
4. "By the end of this video, [specific transformation]."
5. "From zero to [result] in [timeframe] - here's exactly how."

### Authority/Credibility Hooks
1. "After [experience/credential], I've learned that..."
2. "I've taught [number] people to [skill], and they all make this mistake."
3. "[Company/Institution] uses this exact method to..."
4. "As a [role] at [company], here's what actually works."
5. "I analyzed [number] of [things] and found something shocking."

### Story/Anecdote Hooks
1. "Last week, a student asked me something that stopped me cold."
2. "I was about to give up when..."
3. "Picture this: You're [relatable scenario]..."
4. "I'll never forget the day I realized..."
5. "A client came to me with [problem]. Here's what happened next."

## Presentation/Keynote Hooks

### Provocative Statement Hooks
1. "Everything you know about [topic] is wrong."
2. "[Industry/Field] is dead. And that's a good thing."
3. "We've been solving the wrong problem for [time period]."
4. "The future of [field] isn't what you think."
5. "In five years, [prediction]. Here's how I know."

### Statistical/Data Hooks
1. "[Surprising percentage] of [group] fail because of one thing."
2. "We lose [specific amount] every year to [problem]."
3. "[Number] [metric] happen every [time period]. We can fix this."
4. "The numbers don't lie: [surprising statistic]."
5. "By [year], [prediction based on data]. Unless we act now."

### Question Hooks
1. "What would you do if [hypothetical scenario]?"
2. "How many of you have ever [common experience]?"
3. "When was the last time you [action]? Exactly."
4. "Who here believes [common assumption]? You're not alone."
5. "What's the one thing that [outcome]? It's not what you think."

### Historical/Comparison Hooks
1. "In [year], [event] changed everything. It's happening again."
2. "[Historical figure] once said [quote]. They were wrong."
3. "Remember when [past event]? We're making the same mistake."
4. "[Company A] did this and failed. [Company B] did this and won."
5. "100 years ago, we thought [belief]. Today, we know better. Or do we?"

### Personal Vulnerability Hooks
1. "I failed at [thing] for [time] until I realized..."
2. "Three years ago, I was [situation]. Today..."
3. "I'm about to tell you something I've never shared publicly."
4. "My biggest mistake taught me [lesson]."
5. "I used to believe [belief]. I was completely wrong."

## Universal Hook Patterns

### The Pattern Interrupt
- Start with unexpected sound/visual
- Begin mid-action
- Use an unusual word or phrase
- Break the fourth wall immediately
- Subvert genre expectations

### The Knowledge Gap
- Pose a question without immediate answer
- Present incomplete information
- Show effect before cause
- Tease revelation
- Create mystery

### The Emotional Trigger
- Start with universal fear
- Tap into shared frustration
- Evoke nostalgia
- Trigger curiosity
- Appeal to aspiration

### The Social Connection
- Reference shared experience
- Create us vs. them dynamic
- Invoke FOMO
- Build community
- Establish insider knowledge

## Hook Testing Criteria

Before using a hook, verify it:
1. **Grabs attention** in under 5 seconds
2. **Relates** to target audience immediately  
3. **Promises value** explicitly or implicitly
4. **Feels natural** when spoken aloud
5. **Avoids clich√©s** and overused patterns
6. **Sets appropriate tone** for content
7. **Connects to main message** organically

## What NOT to Do

### Avoid These Overused Openers
- "Welcome to my channel/podcast/presentation"
- "In today's video/episode/talk"
- "Hi everyone, I hope you're having a great day"
- "Before we begin, please like and subscribe"
- "Thank you so much for having me"
- "I'm excited/honored/thrilled to be here"
- "Without further ado"
- "Let's dive right in"
- "As you all know"
- "Webster's Dictionary defines..."

### Skip These Weak Patterns
- Apologizing for anything
- Explaining technical difficulties
- Commenting on the weather/venue
- Making inside jokes
- Using vague promises
- Starting with logistics
- Opening with disclaimers
- Beginning with background
- Leading with credentials
- Starting chronologically

## Hook Adaptation Guide

### For Different Lengths
- **5 seconds**: Single provocative statement
- **10 seconds**: Question + promise
- **15 seconds**: Mini-story + transition
- **30 seconds**: Problem + solution preview
- **60 seconds**: Full narrative hook

### For Different Audiences
- **Beginners**: Start with relatability
- **Experts**: Lead with controversy
- **Mixed**: Use universal human experience
- **Skeptical**: Open with proof
- **Supportive**: Begin with vision

### For Different Moods
- **Serious**: Data or consequence
- **Inspirational**: Transformation story
- **Educational**: Curiosity gap
- **Entertaining**: Unexpected twist
- **Urgent**: Time-sensitive problem
