# Brand Discovery Questionnaire

## Purpose
This comprehensive questionnaire guides stakeholder interviews during the brand discovery phase. Adapt questions based on project scope and stakeholder role.

## Organization & Vision

### Foundation Questions
1. **Mission & Purpose**
   - What is the organization's reason for existing beyond making money?
   - What problem are you solving that no one else is?
   - If your organization disappeared tomorrow, what would the world lose?

2. **Vision & Aspirations**
   - Where do you see the organization in 5 years? 10 years?
   - What does success look like for this brand?
   - What legacy do you want to leave?

3. **Core Values**
   - What 3-5 values are non-negotiable for your organization?
   - How do these values show up in daily operations?
   - What behaviors demonstrate these values?

## Target Audience

### Primary Audience
1. **Demographics**
   - Age range, location, income level, education
   - Professional status and industry
   - Life stage and family situation

2. **Psychographics**
   - What are their core values and beliefs?
   - What motivates their decisions?
   - What are their aspirations and fears?

3. **Behaviors**
   - How do they currently solve this problem?
   - Where do they seek information?
   - What influences their purchasing decisions?

### Audience Relationship
1. How do you want your audience to feel when they interact with your brand?
2. What transformation do you provide for your customers?
3. What role does your brand play in their lives?

## Competitive Landscape

### Market Position
1. **Direct Competitors**
   - Who are your top 3-5 competitors?
   - What do they do better than you?
   - What do you do better than them?

2. **Differentiation**
   - What makes you genuinely different?
   - What can customers only get from you?
   - What would make someone choose you over alternatives?

3. **Market Gaps**
   - What needs are not being met in your market?
   - What conventions in your industry need challenging?
   - Where do you see opportunities others don't?

## Brand Personality

### Character Traits
1. **Brand as Person**
   - If your brand was a person, how would you describe them?
   - What would they wear? How would they speak?
   - What would their friends say about them?

2. **Personality Spectrum**
   Rate where your brand falls on these spectrums (1-10):
   - Playful ←→ Serious
   - Modern ←→ Classic
   - Mass Market ←→ Premium
   - Simple ←→ Complex
   - Loud ←→ Understated
   - Rebellious ←→ Conventional

3. **Tone of Voice**
   - How should the brand speak to its audience?
   - What words should it always/never use?
   - How formal or casual should communications be?

## Visual Preferences

### Aesthetic Direction
1. **Visual Associations**
   - What images come to mind when you think of your brand?
   - What colors feel right? What colors feel wrong?
   - What visual styles resonate with your vision?

2. **Competitor Aesthetics**
   - What do you like about competitor visuals?
   - What do you want to avoid?
   - How can you stand out visually?

3. **Reference Points**
   - What brands (any industry) have visuals you admire?
   - What art, design, or cultural movements inspire you?
   - What emotions should your visuals evoke?

## Current State Assessment

### Existing Brand Elements
1. **What's Working**
   - What current brand elements must be retained?
   - What gets the most positive feedback?
   - What are you proud of?

2. **What's Not Working**
   - What causes confusion or miscommunication?
   - What feels outdated or misaligned?
   - What gets negative feedback?

3. **Brand Challenges**
   - What perception challenges do you face?
   - What internal alignment issues exist?
   - What implementation obstacles do you encounter?

## Success Metrics

### Measuring Impact
1. **Short-term (6-12 months)**
   - What immediate changes should the brand enable?
   - How will you measure initial success?
   - What quick wins do you need?

2. **Long-term (2-5 years)**
   - What business metrics will the brand impact?
   - How will brand success be evaluated?
   - What behavioral changes do you expect to see?

3. **Qualitative Indicators**
   - What feedback would indicate success?
   - What stories do you want people telling about your brand?
   - How will you know when the brand is working?

## Implementation Context

### Practical Considerations
1. **Resources**
   - What is the budget for brand implementation?
   - Who will manage the brand internally?
   - What skills and tools are available?

2. **Timeline**
   - When does the brand need to launch?
   - What are the critical milestones?
   - What deadlines are fixed vs. flexible?

3. **Stakeholders**
   - Who needs to approve brand decisions?
   - Who will be brand champions?
   - Who might resist changes?

## Open-Ended Exploration

### Creative Exercises
1. **Metaphors**
   - If your brand was an animal, what would it be and why?
   - If your brand was a place, where would it be?
   - If your brand was a song, what genre would it be?

2. **Associations**
   - What three words should people use to describe your brand?
   - What feeling should your brand evoke?
   - What promise does your brand make?

3. **Vision Casting**
   - Describe a day in the life of your ideal customer using your brand
   - What would a perfect brand interaction look like?
   - What would make you proud to see your brand do?

## Interview Tips

### Best Practices
- Start with broad questions, then get specific
- Ask "why" to uncover deeper insights
- Listen for emotions and stories, not just facts
- Look for contradictions that need resolving
- Document exact phrases and language used

### Red Flags to Note
- Lack of alignment between stakeholders
- Unrealistic expectations about brand impact
- Disconnect between aspiration and capability
- Over-focus on competition vs. unique value
- Resistance to necessary change

### Follow-up Questions
- "Can you give me an example?"
- "What makes you say that?"
- "How is that different from [competitor]?"
- "What would that look like in practice?"
- "Why is that important to you?"
