## Voice Characteristics Framework

### Primary Voice Attributes

Choose 3-5 primary characteristics that define your brand voice:

| Characteristic | Definition | In Practice | To Avoid |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Confident** | Self-assured without arrogance | "We know this works because..." | "Obviously," "Everyone knows" |
| **Helpful** | Service-oriented and supportive | "Here's how you can..." | Condescending explanations |
| **Innovative** | Forward-thinking and creative | "Imagine if..." "What if we..." | Jargon, buzzwords |
| **Authentic** | Genuine and transparent | "Honestly," "In our experience" | Corporate speak |
| **Empowering** | Enabling and encouraging | "You can," "You're capable of" | "You should," "You must" |
| **Approachable** | Friendly and accessible | "Let's explore," "We're here to" | Overly casual, unprofessional |
| **Knowledgeable** | Informed and educational | "Research shows," "Based on data" | Lecturing, showing off |
| **Inspiring** | Motivational and visionary | "Together we can," "Imagine" | Empty platitudes |
| **Practical** | Useful and actionable | "Step 1," "The result is" | Theory without application |
| **Trustworthy** | Reliable and honest | "We promise," "You can count on" | Over-promising |

## Vocabulary Guidelines

### Words We Use vs. Words We Avoid

| Category | We Say | We Don't Say | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Greetings** | Hi, Hello, Welcome | Greetings, Salutations | Stay warm and approachable |
| **Actions** | Get started, Try, Explore | Utilize, Leverage, Implement | Keep it simple and clear |
| **Problems** | Challenge, Issue, Hiccup | Problem, Failure, Error | Stay positive and solutions-focused |
| **Solutions** | Fix, Solve, Improve | Remediate, Rectify | Use everyday language |
| **Customers** | You, People, Folks | Users, Clients, Consumers | Be personal and human |
| **Company** | We, Our team, Us | The company, The organization | Be personal and accessible |
| **Features** | Tools, Features, Capabilities | Functionality, Solutions | Be specific and tangible |
| **Benefits** | Helps you, Saves time, Makes easy | Enables, Facilitates, Empowers | Focus on real outcomes |

## Grammar & Style Preferences

### Sentence Structure

**Preferred:**
- Short sentences for clarity (aim for 15-20 words)
- Active voice (80% minimum)
- Conversational contractions (it's, you'll, we're)
- Direct address using "you"
- Parallel construction in lists

**Avoid:**
- Long, complex sentences with multiple clauses
- Passive voice except when necessary
- Formal constructions (it is, you will)
- Third-person references to the reader
- Inconsistent list formats

### Punctuation Style

| Element | Our Style | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Oxford Comma** | Always use | "Fast, reliable, and secure" |
| **Em Dashes** | Sparingly for emphasis | "Our solution‚Äîunlike others‚Äîactually works" |
| **Exclamation Points** | One per piece max | "Welcome to the future!" |
| **Ellipses** | Only for trailing thoughts | "But there's more..." |
| **Semicolons** | Avoid in marketing copy | Use periods instead |

## Tone Variations by Scenario

### Positive Scenarios

**Onboarding/Welcome**
- Warm and excited
- "We're thrilled you're here!"
- Focus on possibilities

**Success/Achievement**
- Celebratory but not over-the-top
- "Great job! You've mastered..."
- Acknowledge effort

**Feature Announcements**
- Enthusiastic and clear
- "We've been working on something special"
- Emphasize benefits

### Neutral Scenarios

**Instructions/Tutorials**
- Clear and patient
- "First, click on... Then..."
- Anticipate confusion points

**Information/Education**
- Knowledgeable but accessible
- "Here's what you need to know"
- Break down complexity

**Maintenance/Updates**
- Matter-of-fact and reassuring
- "We're improving things behind the scenes"
- Set clear expectations

### Challenging Scenarios

**Error Messages**
- Calm and helpful
- "Oops, something's not right"
- Provide clear next steps

**Apologies/Issues**
- Sincere and accountable
- "We're sorry this happened"
- Focus on resolution

**Difficult News**
- Direct but empathetic
- "We need to share some changes"
- Explain reasoning

## Industry-Specific Voice Adaptations

### Technology/Software
```
Spectrum Position: Innovative but Accessible
Key Traits: Smart, Helpful, Clear
Avoid: Jargon overload, Assumption of expertise
Example: "Powerful features, simply explained"
```

### Education/E-Learning
```
Spectrum Position: Knowledgeable but Approachable  
Key Traits: Encouraging, Patient, Clear
Avoid: Condescension, Overwhelm
Example: "Let's learn this together, step by step"
```

### Healthcare/Wellness
```
Spectrum Position: Professional but Caring
Key Traits: Trustworthy, Empathetic, Clear
Avoid: Cold clinical tone, False promises
Example: "Your health journey, supported"
```

### Financial Services
```
Spectrum Position: Expert but Transparent
Key Traits: Trustworthy, Clear, Confident
Avoid: Complexity, Aggressive sales
Example: "Your money, explained simply"
```

## Voice Consistency Checklist

### Quick Review Questions

Before publishing any content, ask:

- [ ] Does this sound like us?
- [ ] Is the tone appropriate for the context?
- [ ] Have we used our preferred vocabulary?
- [ ] Is the reading level appropriate?
- [ ] Does it reflect our brand values?
- [ ] Would our audience relate to this?
- [ ] Is it clear and actionable?
- [ ] Have we avoided jargon and clich√©s?

### Voice Audit Criteria

Rate each piece of content on:

1. **Personality Match** (1-5)
   - Does it reflect our brand characteristics?

2. **Consistency** (1-5)
   - Does it align with other content?

3. **Clarity** (1-5)
   - Is the message clear and understandable?

4. **Engagement** (1-5)
   - Does it connect with the audience?

5. **Authenticity** (1-5)
   - Does it feel genuine and true to brand?

## Implementation Tips

### Training Your Team

1. **Create Voice Samples**
   - Before/after examples
   - Do's and don'ts
   - Real content rewrites

2. **Provide Templates**
   - Email templates
   - Social media formats
   - Response frameworks

3. **Regular Practice**
   - Voice workshops
   - Peer reviews
   - Content audits

### Maintaining Consistency

1. **Documentation**
   - Keep voice guide updated
   - Document decisions
   - Share examples

2. **Review Process**
   - Designated voice champions
   - Regular audits
   - Feedback loops

3. **Evolution**
   - Monitor audience response
   - Adjust based on data
   - Stay relevant

## Voice Evolution Framework

### Maturity Stages

**Stage 1: Definition**
- Establish basic voice
- Document guidelines
- Train team

**Stage 2: Consistency**
- Apply across channels
- Refine based on usage
- Build recognition

**Stage 3: Differentiation**
- Unique voice emerged
- Audience expects it
- Competitive advantage

**Stage 4: Evolution**
- Adapt to new contexts
- Maintain core identity
- Lead the conversation
