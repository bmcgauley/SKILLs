# Brand Positioning Templates

## Core Positioning Frameworks

### 1. Classic Positioning Statement

**Template:**
```
For [target audience]
Who [statement of need/opportunity]
[Brand name] is [product/service category]
That [key benefit/differentiator]
Unlike [primary competitive alternative]
[Brand name] [primary differentiation]
```

**Example:**
```
For busy professionals
Who need healthy meals but lack time to cook
FreshPrep is a meal delivery service
That provides chef-designed, ready-to-eat meals
Unlike traditional meal kits
FreshPrep requires zero preparation or cleanup
```

### 2. Value Proposition Canvas

**Template:**
```
We help [target customer]
Achieve [desired outcome]
By [your solution/method]
Unlike [alternative]
We [unique differentiator]
```

**Example:**
```
We help small business owners
Achieve professional branding
By providing AI-powered design tools
Unlike expensive agencies
We deliver enterprise-quality results at startup prices
```

### 3. Jobs-to-be-Done Framework

**Template:**
```
When [situation/context]
I want to [job to be done]
So I can [desired outcome/benefit]
```

**Example:**
```
When I'm planning educational content
I want to structure complex topics effectively
So I can maximize student comprehension and engagement
```

### 4. Elevator Pitch Structure

**Template (30 seconds):**
```
You know how [problem/pain point]?
Well, [brand name] [solution].
In fact, [proof point/credibility].
```

**Example:**
```
You know how most online courses have terrible completion rates?
Well, LearnFlow uses AI to adapt content to each learner's pace and style.
In fact, our students show 3x higher completion rates than industry average.
```

### 5. One-Liner Positioning

**Template Options:**

**Outcome-focused:**
`[Brand] helps [audience] achieve [outcome]`

**Problem-focused:**
`[Brand] solves [problem] for [audience]`

**Method-focused:**
`[Brand] uses [method] to help [audience] with [goal]`

**Examples:**
- "Notion helps teams organize everything in one place"
- "Grammarly eliminates writing errors for professionals"
- "Headspace uses meditation to help people reduce stress"

## Differentiation Strategies

### 1. Feature-Based Differentiation
Focus on unique product capabilities or specifications
- "The only platform with real-time collaboration"
- "Twice the battery life of any competitor"
- "Built-in AI that learns your preferences"

### 2. Benefit-Based Differentiation
Emphasize unique outcomes or results
- "Get results in half the time"
- "The only solution that guarantees success"
- "Peace of mind included"

### 3. Process-Based Differentiation
Highlight unique methodology or approach
- "Human-verified AI results"
- "Personalized onboarding for every customer"
- "Iterative design based on your feedback"

### 4. Price-Based Differentiation
Position around value or pricing model
- "Enterprise features at startup prices"
- "Pay only for what you use"
- "The accessible premium option"

### 5. Experience-Based Differentiation
Focus on the customer journey or relationship
- "Concierge service at every step"
- "Join a community, not just a platform"
- "Support that actually responds in minutes"

### 6. Values-Based Differentiation
Align with customer beliefs and principles
- "The sustainable choice"
- "Privacy-first by design"
- "Supporting local communities since 1985"

## Positioning Statement Validation

### Checklist for Strong Positioning

**Clarity**
- [ ] Can be understood in under 10 seconds
- [ ] Uses simple, jargon-free language
- [ ] Clearly identifies the target audience

**Differentiation**
- [ ] Highlights genuine uniqueness
- [ ] Addresses unmet needs
- [ ] Distinguishes from alternatives

**Credibility**
- [ ] Claims are believable
- [ ] Backed by evidence or capability
- [ ] Aligns with brand reality

**Relevance**
- [ ] Solves real customer problems
- [ ] Appeals to target emotions
- [ ] Fits market timing

**Memorability**
- [ ] Contains a hook or surprising element
- [ ] Easy to repeat and share
- [ ] Sticks in the mind

### Testing Your Positioning

**Internal Alignment Test**
- Can every employee explain it?
- Does it guide decision-making?
- Is it inspiring to the team?

**Customer Resonance Test**
- Do customers immediately "get it"?
- Does it address their actual needs?
- Would they use these words?

**Competitive Distinction Test**
- Could competitors claim the same thing?
- Is the differentiation meaningful?
- Does it create preference?

**Durability Test**
- Will this be relevant in 3-5 years?
- Can it evolve with the company?
- Does it allow for growth?

## Common Positioning Mistakes

### Avoid These Pitfalls

1. **Generic Claims**
   - "We provide quality service"
   - "Your trusted partner"
   - "Solutions that work"

2. **Feature Laundry Lists**
   - Trying to be everything to everyone
   - Leading with specifications
   - Focusing on what, not why

3. **Inside-Out Thinking**
   - Using internal jargon
   - Emphasizing your process over their outcome
   - Talking about yourself, not customer value

4. **Copycat Positioning**
   - "The Uber of [industry]"
   - Following category conventions
   - Playing it safe

5. **Aspirational Delusion**
   - Claims you can't deliver
   - Positioning for where you want to be
   - Ignoring current limitations

## Positioning Evolution Framework

### Stage 1: Foundation
Focus on basic differentiation and market fit

### Stage 2: Refinement
Sharpen based on customer feedback and competition

### Stage 3: Expansion
Broaden without losing core identity

### Stage 4: Transformation
Evolve positioning for new markets or offerings

### Regular Review Questions
- Is our positioning still accurate?
- Have customer needs shifted?
- Has competition changed the landscape?
- Are we outgrowing our positioning?
- What new opportunities exist?
